# Week 05 - Proposition Day

## Review and Reflect

I enjoyed pretty much every single experiment throughout the first four weeks; learning how to use p5.js and integrating APLs into our work was very fun to figure out. It felt meaningful because I was learning a practical skill which was right up my alley. I’d like to further explore something like visualising “niche” data as there are certain things I think would be interesting to explore a trend. For example, I think visualising the data of Youtube video analytics and understanding what makes certain moments in a video more captivating than others may be of interest. Learning what makes something trend could be both practical and interesting and could even be used outside of this course for future prospects.

## Thematic Focus and Data Source(s)

A specific human experience I would like to explore is the benefits of humanitarianism and socialism from a socioeconomic perspective. For example, visualising the data of what sorts of laws or behaviours makes humanity thrive would be extremely beneficial for societies in the long run as they can use the data as a guideline for what works and doesn’t. I believe there already are many data sources out there that delve into a similar topic, but more in the form of static data from surveys. Because of this, I think being able to work on a project that takes in and projects live data of livelihood and routines might be something more unique. Whether it’s from a more global perspective or personal data, it could still show a visualisation of what habits are beneficial for the person and the people. I think my idea is in the similar vein to other examples of data visualization that were exemplified in class, such as the data visualization of Cholera and how it aided in preventing its spread. I think if we are able to visualize the data of what makes quality of life better, people can take useful insights from it.

## Visualisation & Impact

From this, I think it may look something like a website in the form of a daily questionnaire that people can watch and update live to show that certain habits are beneficial or negative for someone. I want the public audience to be able to understand what sort of median they belong in from the perspective of their lifestyle, and be able to interpret the data in a way that can help them improve their quality of life. However I also want to approach this from a more “current” perspective instead of just simple questions that seem more like a simple one-off survey. For example, I want people to be able to understand that one grocery store might have fresher products than another, and that this may change over time. Overall, I think this project could be interpreted as a “live trivia for everyday life” that may come in handy for small everyday things.
